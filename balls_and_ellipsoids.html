§0where we are

§2.2.1 gave the flat sets — hyperplane and halfspace — proved convex by a linear inequality. §2.2.2 gives the first curved ones: the ball and the ellipsoid. The convexity proof changes weapon, from "scale a linear inequality" to the norm triangle inequality — a second crack at the recurring question, where is nonnegativity used? And this section is more computational: you'll find semi-axes, build P^½, and run membership tests.

§1the euclidean ball
\(B(x_c,r)=\{x \mid \|x-x_c\|_2 \le r\}=\{x \mid (x-x_c)^{\mathsf T}(x-x_c)\le r^2\}\), with \(r>0\) and \(\|u\|_2=(u^{\mathsf T}u)^{1/2}\).B&V §2.2.2 · L1528

xc is the center, r the radius. The two forms are equal — just square both sides. There's also an image form:

\[ B(x_c,r) = \{x_c + r\,u \mid \|u\|_2 \le 1\} \]

— the unit ball, scaled by r and recentered at xc. That picture is the seed of everything in §2.2.2: take a simple unit set, then stretch and shift it.

§2a ball is convex — the triangle inequality

Take x₁, x₂ ∈ B(xc,r) and θ ∈ [0,1]. Measure the convex combination's distance from the center:

Claim: \(B(x_c,r)\) is convexvia triangle inequality + homogeneity
\[ \|\theta x_1+(1-\theta)x_2 - x_c\|_2 = \|\theta(x_1-x_c)+(1-\theta)(x_2-x_c)\|_2 \]
regroup: \(x_c=\theta x_c+(1-\theta)x_c\)
\[ \le \|\theta(x_1-x_c)\|_2 + \|(1-\theta)(x_2-x_c)\|_2 \]
triangle inequality — split the norm of a sum
\[ = \theta\|x_1-x_c\|_2 + (1-\theta)\|x_2-x_c\|_2 \]
homogeneity \(\|\alpha u\|=|\alpha|\,\|u\|\); the crux: \(\theta,1{-}\theta\ge0\Rightarrow|\theta|=\theta\)
\[ \le \theta r + (1-\theta) r = r. \qquad \therefore\ \in B(x_c,r).\ \blacksquare \]
each distance \(\le r\); weights sum to 1
!

Same "where is nonnegativity used?" theme as §2.2.1. Here θ≥0 makes |θ|=θ in the homogeneity step — a negative weight would give |θ|=−θ and wreck the bookkeeping. Triangle inequality splits the sum; homogeneity pulls the scalars out; θ+(1−θ)=1 closes it. The proof uses only norm axioms — which is exactly why §2.2.3's general-norm balls will be convex too.

§3the ellipsoid — P sets the shape
\(\mathcal E=\{x \mid (x-x_c)^{\mathsf T}P^{-1}(x-x_c)\le 1\}\), where \(P=P^{\mathsf T}\succ 0\) (symmetric positive definite).B&V §2.2.2 · L1559

xc is the center; P sets the shape. The geometry lives in P's spectrum:

  • length
    Semi-axis lengths = √λᵢ, where λᵢ are the eigenvalues of P.
  • dir
    Semi-axis directions = the eigenvectors of P. (Keep them straight: eigenvalue → length √λ, eigenvector → direction.)
  • ball ⟺
    A ball is the ellipsoid with P = r²I: then (r²I)⁻¹=(1/r²)I, the form is ‖x−xc‖²/r² ≤ 1 ⟺ ‖x−xc‖ ≤ r, and every √λᵢ = r.

§4image form & degenerate ellipsoids

Just like the ball, the ellipsoid is an affine image of the unit ball:

\[ \mathcal E = \{x_c + Au \mid \|u\|_2 \le 1\}, \qquad A\ \text{square nonsingular.} \]

Taking A symmetric positive definite WLOG, A = P^½ recovers the quadratic form. So the ellipsoid is literally the unit ball stretched by √λᵢ along P's eigenvectors — the matrix P^½ is the stretch.

▱

Degenerate ellipsoid: let A be symmetric PSD but singular. The set goes flat along ker A, its affine dimension drops to rank A — yet it stays convex, because it's still the affine image of a convex set (the unit ball). In Lab 3, slide λ₂ → 0 to see this collapse.

§5expert lens — calculate

C1Three faces of the ball

{‖x−xc‖₂ ≤ r} = {(x−xc)ᵀ(x−xc) ≤ r²} = {xc+ru : ‖u‖₂≤1}. Convex via triangle inequality + homogeneity.

C2Read the ellipsoid off P

P ≻ 0 (positive definite, not merely invertible). Semi-axes √λᵢ; it's a ball exactly when P = r²I.

C3Build the stretch

Image form uses A = P^½. Degenerate case (singular PSD A): affine dimension = rank A, still convex.

C4Membership test

Compute (x−xc)ᵀP⁻¹(x−xc) and compare to 1: = boundary, < interior, > outside.

Main points for quick review

Ball

\(B(x_c,r)=\{\|x-x_c\|_2\le r\}=\{(x-x_c)^{\mathsf T}(x-x_c)\le r^2\}=\{x_c+ru:\|u\|_2\le1\}\). Center \(x_c\), radius \(r>0\).

Ball is convex

Triangle inequality + homogeneity (\(\theta\ge0\Rightarrow|\theta|=\theta\)) — the norm analogue of §2.2.1's proof.

Ellipsoid

\(\{(x-x_c)^{\mathsf T}P^{-1}(x-x_c)\le1\}\), \(P=P^{\mathsf T}\succ0\). Semi-axes \(\sqrt{\lambda_i}\), directions = eigenvectors; ball \(\Leftrightarrow P=r^2I\).

Image form

\(\{x_c+Au:\|u\|_2\le1\}\), \(A=P^{1/2}\): the unit ball stretched by \(\sqrt{\lambda_i}\).

Degenerate

Singular PSD \(A\): affine dimension \(=\operatorname{rank}A\), still convex.
