Polyhedra

Where the catalogue assembles itself: a polyhedron is the intersection of finitely many halfspaces and hyperplanes — the feasible set of linear inequalities and equalities, and the richest set in §2.2.

§0where we are

§2.2.1–§2.2.3 built single convex sets — a halfspace, a ball, a cone. §2.2.4 assembles them: a polyhedron is the intersection of finitely many halfspaces and hyperplanes, exactly the feasible set of a system of linear inequalities and equalities (and of a linear program, §1.2.2). It's the richest example in §2.2, with two big sub-themes to design around: simplexes and the two representations of a polyhedron.

§1the polyhedron — an intersection
\(P=\{x \mid a_j^{\mathsf T}x \le b_j,\ j=1..m,\ \ c_j^{\mathsf T}x = d_j,\ j=1..p\}\)  (2.5).B&V §2.2.4 · L1629

The intersection of finitely many halfspaces (one per ≤) and hyperplanes (one per =). In compact form:

\[ P=\{x \mid Ax \preceq b,\ Cx = d\}\quad(2.6),\qquad A\text{'s rows}=a_j^{\mathsf T},\ \ C\text{'s rows}=c_j^{\mathsf T}. \]
  • convex
    Each halfspace/hyperplane is convex (§2.2.1), and the intersection of convex sets is convex — so every polyhedron is convex.
  • ≼ means
    Componentwise: u ≼ v ⟺ uᵢ ≤ vᵢ for every i — not strict, not lexicographic.
  • polytope
    A bounded polyhedron is a polytope (B&V's convention; some authors flip the two terms).
  • umbrella
    Affine sets (subspaces, hyperplanes, lines), rays, segments, and halfspaces are all polyhedra — each a special case.

§2orthant & simplexes

Nonnegative orthant (Example 2.4): R₊ⁿ = {x | xᵢ ≥ 0} = {x | x ≽ 0}. It's a polyhedron (the n inequalities −xᵢ ≤ 0) and a cone (x ≽ 0, θ ≥ 0 ⟹ θx ≽ 0) — a polyhedral cone. It's unbounded, so a polyhedron need not be a polytope.

A simplex is \(C=\operatorname{conv}\{v_0,\dots,v_k\}=\{\textstyle\sum_i\theta_i v_i \mid \theta\succeq0,\ \mathbf 1^{\mathsf T}\theta=1\}\) (2.7), with \(v_0,\dots,v_k\) affinely independent — affine dimension \(k\).B&V §2.2.4 · L1686

Affinely independent means v₁−v₀, …, vₖ−v₀ are linearly independent. A 1-D simplex is a segment, 2-D a triangle, 3-D a tetrahedron. Two famous ones live one symbol apart:

  • unit
    Unit simplex (0, e₁,…,eₙ): {x ≽ 0, 1ᵀx ≤ 1} — n-dimensional (full).
  • prob
    Probability simplex (e₁,…,eₙ): {x ≽ 0, 1ᵀx = 1} — (n−1)-dimensional, the distributions on n outcomes.

The single symbol 1ᵀx ≤ 1 vs 1ᵀx = 1 drops the dimension by one: the equality pins you to the hyperplane {1ᵀx = 1}.

▦

A simplex is a polyhedron. Its vertex (conv) description can always be rewritten as an inequality description — the mechanism is a change of coordinates (B = [v₁−v₀ ··· vₖ−v₀], a nonsingular A with AB = (I; 0), giving A₁x ≽ A₁v₀). The takeaway, not the algebra: every simplex sits inside the polyhedron family.

§3two representations — H vs V

A polyhedron has two genuinely different descriptions, and choosing between them decides what's tractable:

  • H-form
    Halfspace constraints: {x | Ax ≼ b, Cx = d} — list the bounding halfspaces.
  • V-form
    Vertices: conv{v₁,…,vₖ} — list the corners. (General form (2.9) adds a conic hull for unbounded directions: conv{v₁..vₘ} + conichull{vₘ₊₁..vₖ}.)

Every polyhedron has both, but converting between them is hard except in special cases (like a simplex). And the sizes can differ exponentially. Take the ℓ∞ box {x | |xᵢ| ≤ 1}:

§4expert lens — design

D1Define it cleanly

Polyhedron = {Ax ≼ b, Cx = d} = finite intersection of halfspaces (≤) and hyperplanes (=). Convex; ≼ componentwise; bounded ⟹ polytope.

D2Recognize the family

Affine sets, rays, segments, halfspaces, and the nonnegative orthant R₊ⁿ (a polyhedral cone) are all polyhedra.

D3Read a simplex

conv of k+1 affinely independent points, affine dim k. Unit {x≽0, 1ᵀx≤1} (n-dim); probability {x≽0, 1ᵀx=1} ((n−1)-dim).

D4Pick the representation

H-form vs V-form. Converting is hard in general; sizes can differ exponentially — choose the one that keeps your problem tractable.

D5Assemble (2.6)

Collect ≤-rows into A,b, =-rows into C,d; rewrite xᵢ ≥ 0 as −xᵢ ≤ 0 (a row of −I).

Main points for quick review

Polyhedron

\(\{Ax\preceq b,\ Cx=d\}\) = intersection of finitely many halfspaces + hyperplanes; convex; \(\preceq\) componentwise; polytope = bounded.

Family

Affine sets, rays, segments, halfspaces, and \(\mathbf R_+^n=\{x\succeq0\}\) (a polyhedral cone) are all polyhedra.

Simplex

conv of \(k{+}1\) affinely independent points, affine dim \(k\). Unit \(\{x\succeq0,\mathbf 1^{\mathsf T}x\le1\}\) (n-dim); probability \(\{x\succeq0,\mathbf 1^{\mathsf T}x=1\}\) ((n−1)-dim = distributions).

Two representations

H-form (inequalities) vs V-form (vertices). Converting is hard; sizes differ exponentially — ℓ∞ box: \(2n\) ineqs vs \(2^n\) vertices.
