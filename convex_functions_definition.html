§0where we are

Chapter 2 built convex sets; Chapter 3 studies convex functions, and §3.1.1 is the gateway definition the whole chapter rests on. Read it for exact statements — the two clauses of the definition and the line-restriction criterion are the load-bearing core.

§1the definition — two clauses
A function \(f:\mathbb{R}^n\to\mathbb{R}\) is convex if (i) \(\operatorname{dom}f\) is a convex set, and (ii) for all \(x,y\in\operatorname{dom}f\) and \(0\le\theta\le1\), \[ f(\theta x+(1-\theta)y)\ \le\ \theta f(x)+(1-\theta)f(y). \]B&V §3.1.1 · Eq. 3.1 · L3383–3386

Both clauses are required. Clause 1 — the convex domain — is the one most often dropped, and it is not implied by clause 2. Geometrically (Fig. 3.1): the chord from \((x,f(x))\) to \((y,f(y))\) lies on or above the graph of \(f\).

!

Direction is the whole story: convex = chord above = "\(\le\)". The inequality quantifies over every pair \(x,y\) in the domain and every mixing weight \(\theta\in[0,1]\).

§2strict · concave · affine
  • strict
    Strictly convex: strict \(<\) in (3.1) whenever \(x\neq y\) and \(0<\theta<1\). Strictness is only meaningful on the open interval, at distinct points — never at the endpoints \(\theta\in\{0,1\}\) or when \(x=y\), where (3.1) is an equality by force.L3390–3395
  • concave
    Concave: \(f\) is concave iff \(-f\) is convex — the chord lies on or below the graph, i.e. \(\ge\) in (3.1). Strictly concave mirrors strictly convex.
  • affine
    Affine \(f(x)=a^{\mathsf T}x+b\) attains equality in (3.1), so it is both convex and concave. Conversely, convex \(\wedge\) concave \(\Rightarrow\) affine — affine functions are the only family that is both.
i

Affine is the boundary case: equality in the chord inequality. It is the unique family sitting on the fence between convex and concave.

§3restriction to a line
\(f\) is convex \(\iff\) for all \(x\in\operatorname{dom}f\) and all \(v\), the function \(g(t)=f(x+tv)\) is convex (on its domain \(\{t\mid x+tv\in\operatorname{dom}f\}\)).B&V §3.1.1 · L3396–3415

This reduces an \(n\)-dimensional convexity question to a 1-D question on every line. The quantifier is all \(x\), all \(v\): a single bad line — where \(g\) fails to be convex — kills convexity of \(f\).

Why (⟸) recovers both clausesline ⟹ full definition
\[ x,y\in\operatorname{dom}f,\ v=y-x:\quad \operatorname{dom}g\ \text{convex interval} \ni 0,1 \]
clause 1: \(g\) convex needs \(\operatorname{dom}g\) convex, so \(\theta x+(1-\theta)y\in\operatorname{dom}f\)
\[ g\ \text{convex}\Rightarrow g(\theta\!\cdot\!0+(1-\theta)\!\cdot\!1)\le\theta g(0)+(1-\theta)g(1) \]
clause 2: since \(g(0)=f(x),\,g(1)=f(y)\), this is exactly (3.1)
!

Diagnostic power: to test convexity, sweep the direction. If every line-restriction is convex, \(f\) is convex; find one line where it bends the wrong way and you are done.

§4why the convex-domain clause is not redundant

If \(\operatorname{dom}f\) is non-convex, there exist \(x,y\in\operatorname{dom}f\) with \(\theta x+(1-\theta)y\notin\operatorname{dom}f\). There \(f\) is simply undefined, so the chord inequality can't even be stated — and \(f\) is not convex regardless of how it behaves where it is defined. Check domain-convexity first.

§5worked examples
Example 1 — \(t^2\) straight from (3.1)strictly convex

For \(f(t)=t^2\) on \(\operatorname{dom}f=\mathbb{R}\) (convex), compute the gap:

\[ \theta x^2+(1-\theta)y^2-\big(\theta x+(1-\theta)y\big)^2=\theta(1-\theta)(x-y)^2\ \ge\ 0. \]

So (3.1) holds, with strict inequality whenever \(x\neq y\) and \(0<\theta<1\) — \(t^2\) is strictly convex.

Example 2 — \(x_1^2\) via the line criterionconvex

Let \(f(x)=x_1^2\) on \(\mathbb{R}^n\). Restrict to a line \(x+tv\):

\[ g(t)=(x_1+tv_1)^2=v_1^2\,t^2+2x_1v_1\,t+x_1^2, \]

a 1-D quadratic with leading coefficient \(v_1^2\ge0\) — convex in \(t\) for every \(x\) and \(v\) (when \(v_1=0\), \(g\) is constant, still convex). By the restriction criterion, \(f\) is convex.

Example 3 — affine is convex ∧ concavethe boundary family

For \(f(x)=a^{\mathsf T}x+b\):

\[ f(\theta x+(1-\theta)y)=\theta(a^{\mathsf T}x+b)+(1-\theta)(a^{\mathsf T}y+b)=\theta f(x)+(1-\theta)f(y). \]

Equality in (3.1) means both \(\le\) and \(\ge\) hold, so \(f\) is convex and concave at once.

Example 4 — non-example: a non-convex domainnot convex

Take \(f(x)=x^2\) but restrict the domain to \(\operatorname{dom}f=\{x\in\mathbb{R}:|x|\ge1\}\) (two rays). The inequality holds wherever \(f\) is defined, yet \(\operatorname{dom}f\) is not convex: with \(x=-1,\ y=1\), the midpoint \(0\notin\operatorname{dom}f\). Clause 1 fails, so \(f\) is not convex — the chord test never gets a chance to run.

§6continuity (stated)
A convex function is continuous on the relative interior of its domain; discontinuities can occur only on the relative boundary.B&V §3.1.1 · L3416–3419

Stated here without proof — a first hint that convexity buys strong regularity for free, everywhere except possibly on the thin relative boundary of the domain.

§7expert lens — diagnose

D1Two clauses

Definition = dom f convex and the chord inequality (3.1). Check the domain first — clause 1 is the one people drop, and clause 2 doesn't imply it.

D2Direction

Convex = chord above = ≤. Concave = −f convex = chord below = ≥.

D3Strictness

Strict < only for x ≠ y and 0 < θ < 1 — never at the endpoints.

D4Affine boundary

Affine = equality in (3.1) ⟹ both convex and concave; and convex ∧ concave ⟹ affine. The only such family.

D5Line restriction

Convex ⟺ convex on every line: quantifier all x, all v. One bad line kills it.

D6In-scope tools

Prove convexity from (3.1) or the line restriction. Gradient/Hessian tests are §3.1.3–4 — not yet available.

Summary · 2026-07-06 · bv.3.1.1

Main points for quick review

Definition

\(f\) convex \(\iff\) \(\operatorname{dom}f\) convex AND \(f(\theta x+(1-\theta)y)\le\theta f(x)+(1-\theta)f(y)\) \(\forall x,y\in\operatorname{dom}f,\ \theta\in[0,1]\). Chord lies above the graph.

Strict · concave

Strictly convex: strict for \(x\neq y,\ 0<\theta<1\). Concave: \(-f\) convex.

Affine

Affine \(\iff\) equality in (3.1) \(\Rightarrow\) both convex and concave; convex \(\wedge\) concave \(\Rightarrow\) affine.

Restriction criterion

\(f\) convex \(\iff\) \(g(t)=f(x+tv)\) convex on every line (all \(x\), all \(v\)).

Continuity

Convex functions are continuous on \(\operatorname{relint}\operatorname{dom}f\).
