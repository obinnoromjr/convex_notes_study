Proper cones &
generalized inequalities

A special kind of cone — a proper cone — turns "greater than" into a partial order on Rⁿ. It unifies two orders you've already used: componentwise ≼ (the orthant) and the Löwner ≽ (the PSD cone).

§0where we are

§2.4 generalizes the scalar order ≤ on R to a partial order on Rⁿ (or Sⁿ) induced by a cone. You've already used two instances without naming them: the componentwise ≼ of §2.2.4 (the orthant) and the matrix/Löwner ≽ of §2.2.5 (the PSD cone). §2.4.1 unifies them — a proper cone induces a generalized inequality that behaves much like ≤.

§1proper cones — all four conditions
A cone \(K\subseteq\mathbf R^n\) is proper if it is convex, closed, solid (nonempty interior), and pointed (contains no line: \(x\in K,\ -x\in K\Rightarrow x=0\)).B&V §2.4.1 · L2208
  • convex+closed
    Convex, and includes its boundary.
  • solid
    Nonempty interior — full-dimensional, not squashed into a lower flat. Makes the strict order nonempty.
  • pointed
    A genuine "tip," not opening into opposite directions. Makes the order antisymmetric.

§2the generalized inequality
\(x\preceq_K y \iff y-x\in K\), and strictly \(x\prec_K y \iff y-x\in\operatorname{int}K\).B&V §2.4.1 · L2214

"x is ≼_K y when the gap y − x points into the cone." Mind the direction — it's y − x, not x − y. The prototype: with K = R₊, y − x ∈ R₊ means y − x ≥ 0, i.e. x ≤ y — the usual scalar order. Equivalently, x ≼_K y ⟺ y ∈ x + K, the up-set of x.

§3the three worked orders
  • 2.14
    Orthant → componentwise. K = Rⁿ₊: x ≼_K y ⟺ xᵢ ≤ yᵢ for all i. Strict: xᵢ < yᵢ for all i. (The ≼ of §2.2.4.)
  • 2.15
    PSD cone → Löwner. K = Sⁿ₊: X ≼_K Y ⟺ Y − X is PSD; strict ⟺ Y − X is PD. This IS the order of §2.2.5 — not an entrywise comparison.
  • 2.16
    Polynomials ≥ 0 on [0,1]. K = {c | c₁ + c₂t + ··· + cₙtⁿ⁻¹ ≥ 0 ∀ t ∈ [0,1]}: c ≼_K d ⟺ poly_c(t) ≤ poly_d(t) for all t ∈ [0,1].

§4why it behaves like ≤ — axiom by axiom

≼_K shares the algebra of ≤: preserved under addition and nonnegative scaling, transitive, reflexive, antisymmetric, preserved under limits. Each property traces back to a cone axiom:

  • cone
    Convex + closed under nonnegative scaling → addition (x ≼ y, u ≼ v ⟹ x+u ≼ y+v) and nonneg scaling.
  • closed
    → preserved under limits.
  • pointed
    → antisymmetric (x ≼ y and y ≼ x ⟹ x = y).
  • solid
    → int K nonempty → the strict order ≺_K is nonvacuous.
→

Forward (§2.4.2, not today): unlike ≤ on R, a generalized inequality is generally not a total order — two points can be incomparable (neither x ≼_K y nor y ≼_K x). That's why "minimum" and "minimal" will become genuinely different.

§5expert lens — diagnose

D1Test properness

Convex + closed + solid (nonempty interior) + pointed (no line). All four — miss one and the order misbehaves.

D2Read the order

x ≼_K y ⟺ y − x ∈ K (direction matters); strict via int K. K = R₊ recovers ≤ / <.

D3The two workhorses

Orthant → componentwise; PSD cone → matrix/Löwner (Y−X PSD; strict = PD) — not entrywise.

D4The diagnose move

To test x ≼_K y, form y − x and check membership in K — ≽ 0 componentwise, or PSD, or polynomial ≥ 0 on [0,1].

Main points for quick review

Proper cone

convex + closed + solid (nonempty interior) + pointed (no line; \(x\in K,-x\in K\Rightarrow x=0\)). All four.

Generalized inequality

\(x\preceq_K y\iff y-x\in K\) (direction matters); strict via \(\operatorname{int}K\). \(K=\mathbf R_+\) recovers \(\le/<\).

Two workhorses

Orthant \(\mathbf R^n_+\) → componentwise (\(x_i\le y_i\ \forall i\)); PSD cone \(S^n_+\) → Löwner (\(Y-X\) PSD; strict = PD) — not entrywise. Third: polynomials ≥ 0 on [0,1].

Behaves like ≤

Addition, transitivity, nonneg scaling, reflexive, antisymmetric, limits. pointed ⟹ antisymmetric, closed ⟹ limits, solid ⟹ strict order nonvacuous. But (§2.4.2) not a total order.
